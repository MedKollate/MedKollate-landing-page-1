FAQs

  1. What is MedKollate?

    2. MedKollate is an EHR service that allows you to collect, collate, and store your personal health and medical records so that you can access them from anywhere, at any time.

  2. What is an EHR?

    3. An Electronic Health Record (EHR) is a digital representation of a patient's medical information that can be used to manage their medical history. It includes everything you would find on a paper chart, plus more.

  3. Why choose MedKollate?

    4. MedKollate gives registered health institutions and their patients instant access to their personal health information 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, from any location with an internet connection.

  4. Who can use MedKollate?

    5. MedKollate services can be primarily used by Hospitals both public and private, Primary Health, Health start-ups and the registered patients of these institutions using our EHR system.

  5. How do I create an account?

    6. Only registered patients of our clients (health institutions using our EHR system) can have an account with us and it is created by the registered health institution who provides their patients with the required details to login. Registered patients of our registered health institutions can successfully create an account by entering a secure username, password, and ID number provided by the hospital. After that, they only need their username and password to sign in.

  6. What kind of health information can I access?

    7. You can access the following health information about yourself:

    • Recent doctor visits
    • Past medical history
    • Vital signs
    • Discharge summaries
    • Diagnosis
    • Medications
    • Immunizations and their respective dates
    • Allergies
    • Lab reports and so on.
  7. If I switch hospitals, will I need to create a new account?

    8. No, your record remains intact as long as the hospital you are switching to is registered on our platform. However, for the above to be possible the registered patient will have to contact our support channel through their service provider that is their registered hospital to tender their request before it can be approved.

  8. Who can access my records apart from me?

    9. The following people can have access to your records:

    1. General Practitioners, specialists, and hospital personnel are examples of healthcare providers.
    2. People who manage your records for you if you are not able to (authorised representatives). MEDKollate is intended for use by everyone engaged in the patient's treatment, including the patient.
  9. Is MEDKollate secure?

    10. Yes, it is. Knowing fully that one of the ethics of health is confidentiality, our clients data are securely safe.
